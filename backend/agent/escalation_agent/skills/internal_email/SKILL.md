# Internal Escalation Email

Use this skill when the email's recipient is inside the company — a team or
colleague such as IT support, sales, marketing, engineering, or operations —
not an external customer or account.

Pick exactly one tone based on the situation, then draft a complete email
(Subject + body) following that tone's structure.

## Tone 1: Urgent / Direct

**When to use**: A critical, time-sensitive issue that needs immediate
action — an outage, a blocking bug, a deadline at risk.

- **Subject**: `[URGENT] <one-line problem>` — state the problem, not just the issue ID.
- **Opening**: Lead with the impact and the ask, in the first sentence — no pleasantries.
- **Body**: Bullet the facts (what's broken, since when, who's affected); bold the specific action needed and by when.
- **Closing**: Name the owner and deadline explicitly. Short sign-off.

## Tone 2: Collaborative / Informative

**When to use**: A status update or a request for input among peers — no
immediate crisis, more about keeping people in the loop or gathering
context.

- **Subject**: `Update: <topic>` or `Question: <topic>`.
- **Opening**: Brief context/greeting, then the purpose of the email.
- **Body**: Narrative paragraph(s) explaining what's happened and what's needed from the reader; invite discussion or questions.
- **Closing**: Friendly, open-ended ("let me know your thoughts").

## Tone 3: Formal / Procedural

**When to use**: Something that needs to go on record — compliance,
cross-departmental sign-off, an escalation that may be referenced later.

- **Subject**: `Escalation: Issue #<id> — <title>`.
- **Opening**: Formal salutation, state the purpose and reference the issue ID/ticket up front.
- **Body**: Structured sections (Summary, Current Status, Requested Action, Owner), factual and precise, no informal language.
- **Closing**: Formal sign-off with name/role.
