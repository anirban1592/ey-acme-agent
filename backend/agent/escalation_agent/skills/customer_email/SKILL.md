# Customer Escalation Email

Use this skill when the email's recipient is an external customer or
account — e.g. Deloitte, Google, Facebook — not an internal team.

Pick exactly one tone based on the situation, then draft a complete email
(Subject + body) following that tone's structure.

## Tone 1: Empathetic / Reassuring

**When to use**: The customer is experiencing frustration, a service
disruption, or a delay — the priority is acknowledging impact and
reassuring them action is underway.

- **Subject**: `Update on your reported issue` or similar, non-alarming.
- **Opening**: Acknowledge the impact on them first, before any explanation.
- **Body**: Plain language, no internal jargon or ticket-system terms; explain what's being done in customer-facing terms.
- **Closing**: Offer a named point of contact and a next-update timeframe.

## Tone 2: Professional / Formal

**When to use**: A strategic/enterprise account that expects formal written
status updates (e.g. an account like Deloitte, per its account notes) —
procurement- or compliance-sensitive relationships.

- **Subject**: `Status Update: <Account Name> — <topic>`.
- **Opening**: Formal salutation, addressed to the named primary contact.
- **Body**: Structured like a status report — current status, what has been done, what's next — references the account manager/support lead by name.
- **Closing**: Formal sign-off, contact details for follow-up.

## Tone 3: Proactive / Transparent

**When to use**: Notifying the customer of something before they've asked —
a known risk, a pending renewal, a heads-up about a change — building trust
through transparency rather than reacting to a complaint.

- **Subject**: `Heads-up: <topic>` or `A quick update on <topic>`.
- **Opening**: State plainly why you're reaching out now, unprompted.
- **Body**: Explain the situation honestly, including any risk or uncertainty, and what the customer can expect next.
- **Closing**: Invite questions, offer a clear point of contact.
